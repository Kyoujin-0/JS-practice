What resembles me

  • Life is mostly about trying things out and figuring out what works. Games, coding, and music are part of that process for me. Games are not just a way to pass the time; they’re a mix of challenge and distraction. They were a drug of silence and a peace that hooks me; it makes me drown but it doesn’t hurt my lungs. I was fascinated by games, so I chose to take this course, and maybe you could even see me one day creating a game too, as my project or as an activity for a school requirement.
  • Coding is different. Coding is where things actually get built. It can be frustrating, and sometimes nothing works the way it should. I don’t necessarily enjoy coding, but I’ve realized it was something that just needs to be done and something that I must excel at since I already put myself into this situation. Anyways, seeing a program do what it’s supposed to do is satisfying. It’s a quiet kind of reward that doesn’t need applause.
  • Music, on the other hand, serves a slightly different purpose. Sometimes it’s just background, but often it’s a way to shut down overthinking. Listening to something loud, simple, or even absurdly catchy can completely numb the brain for a while, which is convenient when my thoughts start spiraling. In other words, it’s a built-in dumb mode switch. It makes me feel relaxed at times or motivated when I need to push through.

This page isn’t a resume or a list of accomplishments. It’s a snapshot of what takes up a few hours of my day and keeps things moving. Games, coding, and music aren’t everything about me, but they’re part of the structure that organizes how I spend my time.
